Smorrebrod Recipe

Hillary Food, Recipes Danish food, pumpernickel bread, salmon toast, Scandinavian food, smoked salmon toast, smorrebrod, toast 1 Comment

My favorite recipes usually start out as not-recipes. They emerge from solitary nights of unplanned eating, when no one else is around. Usually, when I’m cooking, it’s for a crowd, or at least it’s dinner for me and Dan. Once in a while, though, Dan’s got plans after work and I’m too ashamed to admit to my friends that I’m convinced Robert Durst is hiding in my shower (as if a Durst would ever take the 7 train), so I don’t invite anybody over to keep me company. And I end up eating a loaf of bread by myself.

Sometimes I just spread a slice of toast with peanut butter. Other times it’s a smear of mayonnaise and a chopped up, boiled egg. Radish and butter is a favorite. So is sour cream and smoked salmon, or tuna salad and pickles. It’s an exercise in fridge-raiding that I’ve perfected through the years. Anchovy, hot sauce and mashed potatoes on a slice of sourdough? Don’t mind if I do.

I never would have thought to serve these toasts to anyone. It’s always felt like my food, less in a possessive way, but more in an intimate way.

Intimacy, shmintimacy. Turns out I’m not the only person who eats open-faced sandwiches as lazy comfort food. I’d been minding my own business, eating my bread-with-stuff-on-top dinner all this time and then I find out that my “recipes” are, like, a major staple of Scandinavian cuisine. This is breakfast in Sweden and Denmark and Norway. This is their oatmeal.

When I learned this, I felt exposed for a second and a little embarrassed that I assumed, basically, toast was my thing. It sounds absurd saying it out loud.

But then I realized: this means I can make my favorite “recipe” for people and it won’t seem like a totally lazy move. Just tell your guests that you’re serving a Danish smørrebrød. Bam! Brunch is ready in ten minutes, everyone feels kind of fancy and you never had to turn your oven on.

I based the spread on this recipe from Epicurious.

Smorrebrod Recipe

For the sour cream spread

3/4 cup sour cream

1 Tablespoon chopped parsley

1 Tablespoon chopped dill

1 Tablespoon lemon juice

1 1/2 Tablespoons grated horseradish

salt, pepper

For the sandwiches

8 slices rye or pumpernickel bread

12 ounces smoked salmon or lox

2 radishes

salt, pepper

extra dill and parsley for garnish

1. Whisk together ingredients for sour cream spread. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

2. If you’re OK with going the inauthentic route that tastes better IMO, toast your slices of bread in a 400 degree oven for 2 minutes, placing bread slices directly on rack. (Optional. In Denmark, bread is definitely not toasted.)

3. Spread about a tablespoon of sour cream mixture onto a slice of bread, using the back of a spoon. Repeat for all bread slices.

4. Top each toast with a slice of salmon. Then, garnish with radish slices and remaining herbs and serve!
